.Bioliteracy: A basic understanding of biological concepts, a knowledge of how these concepts were arrived at, & the ability to use them to evaluate, communicate and generate new ideas.

Every day we face a stream of news that describes biological breakthroughs, dangers and possibilities.  Dramatic announcements of an increased danger of, or protection from disease are commonplace. 

Biology-based debates range from the benefits and risks (pdf) of genetic engineering and stem cells, pesticides, organic foods, irradiation, nutriceuticals, alternative therapies, and childhood vaccination to the validity of the theory of evolution.
 

Surprisingly, such claims are often based on small preliminary studies that are later abandoned or retracted with little or no fanfare. That is, of course, if any objective evidence exists to support these claims at all.

The outcomes of these debates impact both public policy and personal decisions. Yet, the public is often unaware or misinformed about basic scientific terms, principles, and methods.
 

The end result can be flawed, dangerous, misdirected and wasteful decisions, scientifically frivolous lawsuits, wasted resources and missed opportunities, as well costly and potentially tragic personal choices.

Given the demonstrated power of science and science-based technologies, all kinds of groundless or problematic assertions are clothed in scientific legitimacy.

 
 

Even actively anti-scientific/anti-rationalist groups (which are depressingly common on both the extreme left and the extreme right of the political spectrum) use science's authority to bolster their claims.

At the same time many studies reveal the general public's alarming level of ignorance and misunderstanding not only of biological science, but of the scientific process in general.

The lack of basic bioliteracy leaves the public at the mercy of those with covert social, political or economic agendas rather than solid science in support of their position.

The public is ill-prepared to sort the wheat from the chaff, to identify sound from problematic claims, not mention those that are simply wishful thinking, cynical misrepresentations, ideological posturing or self-serving hype. 

Basic scientific literacy is a potent first step in arming the public against all manner of hype and misrepresentation.
