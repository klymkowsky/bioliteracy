Using the Biology Concept List to evaluate course content. 

The first hurdle in determining whether a course if effective is to know what the course is designed to teach.  

While this may seem obvious,  it is our experience that course "goals" and content are often poorly conceptualized and difficult to define

A relatively easy way to begin the process of defining  conceptual goals is to make explicit the concepts that you plan to emphasize in your course. 

To facilitate this process, we have generated a
Biology Concept List (download as pdf).

Before you teach your course, we suggest that you go through the BCL and indicate those statements that are emphasized and critical; an interesting exercise is to give the BCL to your students/TAs at the end of the course have have them fill it out - this will let you know whether your perception of the course was shared by others. 

Differences can be enlightening. 

If you send us your BCL (by email or fax: 303-492-7744), it will he-lp us determine what is taught in the typical introductory biology course (an important aspect of our BCI development plan). 

Your completed BCL can also be useful in comparing courses between institutions and for analyzing curriculum content.
