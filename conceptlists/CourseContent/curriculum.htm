Using the Biology Concept List to analyze your curriculum. 

Curriculum design: 

A common complaint of students is that many curricula are highly redundant -- material is "covered" many times (even though, at the end of the process, it is not uncommon to find that many students are still shaky as to their mastery of the materials). 

It is possible to use the BCL to generate a map of curricular content. 

In this method, the number of statements within a topic area determines its map radius; the number of statements "covered" is used to determine the extent to which the circle is filled in.  

Once this analysis has been done for each required course in the curriculum, it is a simple matter to visualize the redudancies and the holes within a curriculum.
