U.S. High School Seniors Not Ready for College Biology (reprinted from the American Society for Cell Biology newsletter, volume 26. Sept. 2003).

ACT scores of current high school seniors paint a grim picture of the scientific preparation of the nation’s high school graduating class of 2003. 
 

The national average composite score remained steady and a record number of students took the exam.

Test results show that a large majority of those taking the tests have not acquired the skills they will need to be prepared for college-level biology courses in the fall.  

  

We recommend you read: Ersatz Learning, Inauthentic Testing. by McClymer & Knoles. 1992. Journal of Excellence in College Teaching 3:33-50.
 
 

Only 26% earned a score of 24 or higher (of a possible 36 points) on the ACT Science Test.  

ACT research  has  shown that students who score 24 or above on the Science test are significantly more likely to be ready for college biology classes than those who score 23 or lower.

 

“We’ve heard a lot of talk recently about the inadequacy of students’ writing skills,” said ACT Chief Executive Officer Richard L. Ferguson.  “However, it appears that the more critical problems are in science and math.”
