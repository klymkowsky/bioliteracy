Using the Biology Concept List (BCL) to evaluate student learning

Help us develop the Biology concept inventory (BCI) : As an instructor you know what you are trying to teach.  To insure that the BCI covers these materials use the Biological Concept Workspace to indicate the concepts you are teach and provide us with feedback about mistakes and omissions. 

Based on these statements and student interviews, we are generating concept test questions and distracters (wrong answers) based on common student misconceptions.

Use the BCL right now in your course: To use the BCL you can download the latest version (pdf or rtf or word versions).   Select those concepts you think are critical and that you emphasize in your course. 

Send us that list (or give it to a colleague).  We will generate as set of 3 to 5 essay questions that your students can answer with short (100-200 word) essays.  These can be answered either on-line through our site or you can collect the written answers. 

These questions should then be analyzed by an independent set of expert graders (you should not be involved in either generating the questions or evaluating the answers!)

By sharing your student's answers with us (no identifiers please), you help us identify misconceptions for use as distractors in the BCI and through our collaboration with Isidoros Doxas and Walter Kinsch (UC Boulder) in the development of an automated evaluation system.
