Essay questions:   We are using these questions to map student's conceptual landscapes, and as jumping-off points for further "talk aloud" discussions.  

    Questions we need answers to...
  Macromolecular interactions tend to be regulated and reversible; describe some of the ways these interactions are regulated.
 

The words "fact", "hypothesis" and "theory" have very specific meanings in science. They are used in ways quite different from common, public speech.

Part 1: Explain to a non-scientist the difference between what scientists mean when they talk about a theory, and what non-scientists mean.

Part 2: Do theories ever become facts? why or why not?
  Explain what is causing the increasing medical problem of antibiotic resistance in bacteria?
  Describe how new strains of old viruses (such as avian flu) and new human viruses (such as HIV and Ebola virus) occurs?
  Protein shape is dictated in large part by the amino acid sequence of the protein. How does an amino acid sequence "know" what shape it should be?
 

Energy is required to promote the polymerization of amino acids into polypeptides. In addition, during the synthesis of a polypeptide, water is generated. Why is energy necessary for polypeptide formation and why don't polypeptides disassemble into amino acids when dissolved in water?
  Plants droop when they dry out, and perk up when watered - why?
  Imagine that you are molecule of ADP inside a cell. Describe how you manage to "find" an ATP synthase, so that you can become an ATP.
  Genetic drift does not occur in asexual species, why is that?
  What features of double-stranded DNA make it particularly suitable for storing information, and ineffectual as a catalyst.
  How can a mutation be creative?  
  How would go about determining whether two genes are located on the same chromosome?
